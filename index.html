Daniella Kubiak

I am a product design engineer and recent graduate of Dartmouth College. I have interned creating weatherproof casing for electronics, designed art tools at the request of friends, and created websites for fun. During my time in college, I also had the opportunity to work on the hardware side of many of my designs. Here are some of my favorite projects that I designed and built. Enjoy!

Touchless Musical Instrument

A theremin with pitch and volume coded with VHDL and completely controlled with motion sensors. The display shows the current sound frequency being outputted as well as LEDs which illuminate corresponding to pitch.

Stirling Engine

A traditional thermal engine controlled entirely by air pressure differentials. Built by lathe, mill, and bandsaw and operated with a blowtorch (!). It consistently runs at 600rpm.

Beam Bridge

Standard beam bridge designed in Solidworks. Frame built from laser-cut 3 ply birch plywood and reinforced lengthwise with rectangular carbon fiber tubing. It supported 10kN of bending force.

No-Spill Ladle

Mechanical ladle designed to eliminate a user's awkward arm movement when pouring. Neck and handle were built by repurposing kitchen equipment and cup designed in Solidworks and 3D printed. Cup was coated with silicon solution to prevent dripping. A motor-run testing apparatus was built and implemented to ensure stability.
